United Arab Emirates Traditions

The United Arab Emirates is a well-known country for its international events, modern infrastructure and status as a trade and transport hub. All things aside, UAE has a lot of different traditions that have been celebrated for decades, for instance their national day, camel racing, falconry, horse riding and the Dubai shopping festival etc.

Falconry is one of the most traditional sports in the United Arab Emirates and has been a tradition for more than 2,000 years. It is still being passed down from generation to generation to keep the tradition alive. It is said that the requirement to be able to control a falcon is to have patience and bond with the bird, so that the both of you can trust each other. For some time now falconers have been able to breed falcons and produce new techniques to manage a falcon. The UAE has a falcon experience that gives you an unimaginable experience of an ancient tradition in person. Falconry was first used as a method for hunting in the past, nowadays falconers breed and train their falcons. But they occasionally take their falcons to exhibitions and events that are taking place all year long in UAE. The national bird of UAE is the falcon, which is the official emblem of the country that consists of a golden falcon. The reason the falcon is the country’s national bird is because it symbolizes courage and strength.

Before the United Arab Emirates became a country, it consisted of rival tribes that ruled various parts of the country. Since the rival tribes were causing problems, Britain offered protection in exchange for some land. The desperation for peace led to countless tribes accepting Britain’s offer in 1820. In 1968 Britain came across lots of financial losses which lead to them withdrawing. On December 2, 1971, the rulers of Abu Dhabi, Dubai, Al-Ain, Sharjah, and Umm al-Quwain agreed on the unification of the country, which resulted in creating the United Arab Emirates. On the same day, the rulers declared the country’s independence from Britain. Ever since that day citizens of UAE highly anticipates the national day which celebrates the unification of United Arab Emirates. The national day is an especially important day since it shows how peace can be achieved without force or wars. The 50th anniversary of being independent was celebrated on December 2, 2021.

When people talk about the United Arab Emirates, they often think about Dubai which is one of the biggest shopping destinations that consists of over seventy malls and still counting, while new ones open every year. Dubai is a shopping district that is mostly made up of exclusive brands and expensive clothing. That is when the Dubai shopping festival comes in and offers exceptional discounts on exclusive brands. Dubai shopping festival initially started on February 16, 1996, as a retail event, which later resulted in becoming a tourist attraction that attracts three million tourists worldwide each year. The Dubai shopping festival lasts for a month where the discounts and sales on products are crazy, the festival starts from the first week of January to the first week of February. The festival is often held throughout Dubai since several malls and retail stores participate. To celebrate the festival some parks, hold different events and light fireworks
